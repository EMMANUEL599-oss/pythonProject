Message Regarding the Upcoming Men's Meeting

Hi cous,

Thank you for extending the invitation to the upcoming men’s meeting regarding our ancestral land boundaries. 📍 I recognize the importance of such gatherings and respect the effort being made to address these matters. 🤝

However, I wish to share my thoughts openly and honestly. 🗣

Earlier this year, in March, my grandmother graciously gave me a small piece of land near my father’s home to build my manyatta house. 🛖 I received this gift with gratitude 🙏 and began planning for the future with hope. 🌱

Unfortunately, when this matter was later discussed among some of my uncles, it was met with denial and disregard — despite my grandmother’s clear intention. ❌

This experience left me deeply disappointed and hurt. 💔 It felt as though my place, voice, and dignity were dismissed. 😔 As someone who respects family and values fairness, I found this treatment unjust and disheartening. ⚖

Because of what was done to me, I have made the difficult decision not to attend the upcoming meeting. 🚫 I do this not out of anger, but as a way of preserving my peace 🕊 and standing firm in what I know to be true. 💪

I remain committed to the unity and well-being of our family ❤ and I hope and pray that one day, we will be able to handle such matters with fairness, respect, and integrity for all — regardless of age or status. 🤲🏾👣

Wishing you a peaceful and constructive meeting. ✨

With respect,
Manu
